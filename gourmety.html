Case Study

Gourmety

Overview

Gourmety is a culinary exploration platform tailored specifically for teenagers, while remaining accessible to all aspiring cooks, regardless of their culinary constraints.

Problem Statement

Nutrition and meal preparation play pivotal roles in our daily lives, yet teenagers today face significant challenges in adopting healthy eating habits due to a multitude of factors. According to a survey by the American Psychological Association, teenagers report feeling overwhelmed by academic pressures, extracurricular activities, and social obligations, leaving them with limited time to dedicate to cooking nutritious meals. Additionally, studies published in the Journal of Nutrition Education and Behavior highlight the lack of basic cooking skills and nutrition knowledge among teenagers, often attributed to a lack of exposure to cooking at home or in school.

Moreover, many teenagers encounter barriers such as limited access to cooking resources, including kitchen equipment and space, especially those residing in dormitories or shared housing. Anecdotal evidence suggests that ingredient availability, particularly in urban areas or food deserts, further exacerbates the challenge of preparing healthy meals. Coupled with a lack of confidence in their cooking abilities and motivation to cook from scratch, teenagers often resort to unhealthy eating habits or rely on processed foods.

Possible Solution

In light of these challenges, there is a pressing need for a solution that empowers teenagers to overcome barriers to cooking and nutrition education. Introducing a comprehensive recipe search feature, equipped with advanced filtering options tailored to the specific constraints faced by teenagers, can serve as a catalyst for change. By providing accessible, nutritious recipes that accommodate limited time, resources, and cooking skills, the Gourmety app aims to revolutionize the way teenagers approach cooking, empowering them to make healthier food choices and develop essential life skills.

Design Process

I adopted a user-centric design methodology, beginning with a thorough comprehension of user needs and preferences. Throughout the design process, emphasis was placed on continually refining and enhancing user-centered concepts. From initial ideation to the final product, iterative design cycles were employed to ensure an optimized user experience at every stage of development.

Understand

In the initial phase of the design process, I conducted comprehensive research to gain insights into user needs and preferences, as well as to understand the competitive landscape. This dual approach allowed me to gather valuable insights from multiple sources, informing the design decisions for the Gourmety app.

User Research Insights

To gain a deeper understanding of the needs and preferences of potential users, I conducted user research through a combination of self-interviews and empathy practices. Through these interviews, I explored questions such as frequency of cooking, challenges faced in meal preparation, and desired features in a cooking app. Key insights from the user research include:

  • Time Constraints: "I often find myself pressed for time when it comes to cooking, especially during busy weekdays."
  • Limited Access to Ingredients and Equipment: "Accessing fresh ingredients and kitchen equipment can be challenging, especially in shared living spaces."
  • Preference for Simplified Recipes: "I prefer recipes that are simple, require minimal ingredients, and are adaptable to my dietary preferences."

Competitive Analysis

In parallel with user research, I conducted a competitive analysis to evaluate existing cooking apps and identify opportunities for differentiation. Through this analysis, I assessed competitors' strengths, weaknesses, gaps, and opportunities. Key findings from the competitive analysis include:

  • Competitors' Strengths: Existing cooking apps offer a wide range of recipes, user-friendly interfaces, and social features such as recipe sharing and meal planning. Notably, competitors like Cookpad and Tasty have integrated features for users to share food photos, fostering a sense of community and inspiration among users.
  • Competitors' Weaknesses: However, many competitors lack robust filtering options based on user constraints such as cookware, cooking time, and dietary preferences. This presents an opportunity to provide a more tailored experience for users with specific needs.
  • Gaps and Opportunities: Additionally, there is a gap in the market for a cooking app that specifically targets teenagers and addresses their unique challenges and preferences. By focusing on this demographic and integrating features such as simplified recipes and kitchen equipment filtering, Gourmety can differentiate itself in the market.

Define

In the Define phase, personas and user journey maps were created to understand the target audience's needs, behaviors, and preferences based on insights gathered during user interviews.

Persona

User Journey Map

Marcus' Journey

  1. Marcus starts his culinary journey by searching for recipes using the Gourmety app.
  2. He navigates through the search results, filtering recipes based on available cooking tools and ingredients.
  3. Marcus selects a recipe that aligns with his preferences and begins cooking in his school dormitory kitchen.
  4. He follows the recipe instructions step-by-step, utilizing the limited equipment and ingredients at his disposal.
  5. After preparing the meal, Marcus shares his cooking success on the Gourmety app and receives positive feedback from the community.

Queena's Journey

  1. Queena accesses the Gourmety app during a break from her hectic schedule.
  2. She explores the latest articles and cooking tips featured on the app's homepage, seeking inspiration for her next meal.
  3. Queena utilizes the search function to find quick and nutritious recipes that fit her dietary preferences and time constraints.
  4. She saves her favorite recipes to her collection for future reference and adds ingredients to her shopping list.
  5. Queena prepares a healthy meal using the recipe instructions provided, enjoying a satisfying and nutritious dining experience.

Persona Insights

  • Through the development of personas, common themes such as the importance of convenience, nutrition, and simplicity in meal preparation emerged.
  • Surprisingly, both Marcus and Queena expressed a desire for community engagement and sharing their cooking experiences with others on the Gourmety app.

User Needs and Goals

  • The primary needs of Marcus and Queena revolve around accessing convenient, nutritious, and easy-to-follow recipes that cater to their specific constraints and preferences.
  • Both personas seek a user-friendly platform that offers personalized recommendations and fosters community engagement and interaction.

Impact on Design Decisions

  • The personas and user journey maps played a crucial role in shaping the design decisions of the Gourmety app, ensuring that the features and functionalities align with the needs and goals of the target users.
  • Insights derived from personas guided the development of tailored features, such as the "What’s in my kitchen" filter and the community sharing functionality, enhancing the overall user experience.

  1. Smart Recipe Search Engine: Find recipes based on available ingredients.
  2. Cookware Selection Cards: Easily choose the right cookware for your recipes with handy selection cards.

Design

In the design phase of the Gourmety app development process, I focused on translating user insights and requirements into tangible design solutions. This phase involved crafting wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes to visualize the app's user interface and refine its functionality.

Wireframes

I began the design process by creating wireframes, which served as a blueprint for the app's layout and interaction flow. These wireframes outlined the key screens and interactions, including the search screen, search results, and recipe pages. Through iterative sketching and refinement, I ensured that the wireframes accurately captured the core features and user flow of the app.

Search Screen Sketch

One of the primary screens I designed was the search screen, which serves as the central hub for recipe discovery. Here, users can input their search criteria and explore a wide range of recipes tailored to their preferences. To streamline the user experience, I retained the "What’s in my kitchen" feature as a filter function, simplifying the search process while maintaining visual engagement through an immersive background design concept.

Search Result Sketch

The search result screen displays a curated list of recipes based on the user's search criteria. I maintained design coherence by incorporating a consistent curved design motif throughout the interface, ensuring visual consistency and branding. Each search result preview provides essential information such as cooking time, difficulty level, and user ratings, allowing users to make informed decisions about which recipe to explore further.

Recipe Page Sketch

The recipe page is where users can view detailed instructions and ingredients for a selected recipe. I introduced interactive elements such as checkbox options for ingredient selection and a "check all" button for added convenience. Additionally, I optimized the instructional section with a "full-screen" button and designated areas for instructional photos, enhancing the usability of the recipe page.

Other Screens and Low-Fidelity Prototype

In addition to the search and recipe pages, I sketched wireframes for other essential screens such as the Today, Collection, Kitchen, Shopping List, and Account screens. These wireframes provided a comprehensive overview of the app's functionality and user flow. I then translated these wireframes into low-fidelity prototypes, allowing stakeholders to interact with the app's basic functionality and flow.

Test

Now, let's see how we checked if our designs really work. We ran tests to see how easy the app is to use. Let's find out what we learned!

Methodology

For the usability testing phase of the Gourmety app, I employed a combination of self-testing and one-participant testing to evaluate the prototype's usability and identify areas for improvement.

Self-Test

As a preliminary step, I conducted a self-test to navigate through the prototype and identify any initial usability issues or inconsistencies. This allowed me to gain firsthand experience with the app's functionality and user interface and make initial observations before involving external participants.

One-Participant Test:

Following the self-test, I conducted a one-participant test with a selected participant to gather additional feedback and insights. The participant, within the target demographic of young people aged 12–25 with cooking experience, completed tasks related to recipe discovery and navigation within the app. The test session lasted approximately 10-15 minutes and was conducted remotely.

Insights and Recommendations

Based on the findings from both the self-test and one-participant test, key insights and recommendations were derived to improve the prototype's usability and user experience. These insights highlight areas of concern such as navigation clarity, information coherence, and icon clarity, providing actionable steps for refinement and enhancement.

Iteration and UI Design

After reviewing the usability test, I identified three key areas that needed improvement: navigation clarity, information coherence, and icon clarity. These insights guided the enhancements made during the UI design phase to ensure a more intuitive and user-friendly experience.

UI Design Sticker Sheet

Navigation Clarity

Based on insights from the usability test, I recognized that the interaction might be confusing for many users. To address this, I introduced a 'Recipe' button to provide clearer guidance and improve user understanding of the app's functionality.

Information Coherence

The usability test highlighted the importance of maintaining information consistency across different app interfaces. As a result, I added a cookware label and a 'like' button to the recipe page, in addition to the search result page. This ensures a cohesive user experience and facilitates easier navigation and interaction.

Icon Clarity

During the UI design stage, I made several adjustments to enhance icon clarity and improve overall user experience. This included revising the navigation tab icon choices and introducing a search icon to the search bar. I replaced the day-check symbol with a newspaper icon and renamed the 'Today' page to the 'News' page for better icon representation. Additionally, adding titles beneath the icons improved user understanding and navigation within the app.

Takeaway

  1. Prioritize User Experience: By conducting thorough user research and usability testing, we were able to identify areas for improvement and make informed design decisions to enhance the user experience.
  2. Iterative Design Process: Our iterative design process allowed us to continually refine and optimize the Gourmety app prototype based on user feedback and insights gathered at each stage of development.
  3. Focus on Clarity and Consistency: Enhancements focused on improving navigation clarity, information coherence, and icon clarity, ensuring that users can easily navigate the app and understand its functionality consistently.
  4. User-Centric Design: The personas and user journey maps created during the design process helped us empathize with our target users and tailor the app's features to meet their specific needs and preferences.
  5. Continuous Improvement: Usability testing served as a valuable tool for validating design decisions and identifying areas for further refinement, highlighting the importance of ongoing iteration and improvement in the design process.
  6. Empowering User Engagement: Features such as the "Recipe" button, cookware labels, and like buttons aim to empower users to engage more effectively with the app, fostering a sense of ownership and satisfaction in their culinary exploration journey.
