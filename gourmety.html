Case Study

Gourmety

Overview

Gourmety is a culinary exploration platform designed specifically for teenagers, while remaining accessible to anyone eager to learn cooking, regardless of their constraints.

Problem Statement

Nutrition and meal preparation are essential parts of daily life, yet teenagers today face significant challenges in adopting healthy eating habits due to various factors. According to a survey by the American Psychological Association, teenagers often feel overwhelmed by academic pressures, extracurricular commitments, and social demands, leaving them little time to focus on preparing nutritious meals. Studies published in the Journal of Nutrition Education and Behavior also reveal a widespread lack of basic cooking skills and nutrition knowledge among teenagers, often stemming from insufficient exposure to cooking at home or school.

Beyond this, many teenagers face additional hurdles, such as limited access to kitchen equipment and space—a common issue for those living in dormitories or shared housing. Anecdotal evidence also points to ingredient availability, especially in urban areas or food deserts, as a significant obstacle to preparing healthy meals. Combined with a lack of confidence in their cooking abilities and motivation to cook from scratch, teenagers frequently resort to unhealthy eating habits or processed foods.

Possible Solution

To address these challenges, there is a clear need for a solution that helps teenagers overcome barriers to cooking and nutrition education. Introducing a comprehensive recipe search feature with advanced filtering options tailored to the unique constraints teenagers face could be transformative. By offering accessible, nutritious recipes that align with limited time, resources, and cooking skills, the Gourmety app aims to empower teenagers to make healthier food choices, build confidence in the kitchen, and develop essential life skills.

Design Process

I adopted a user-centric design methodology, starting with a thorough understanding of user needs and preferences. Throughout the design process, I focused on continuously refining and improving user-centered ideas. From initial ideation to the final product, I implemented iterative design cycles to ensure an optimized user experience at every stage of development.

Understand

In the initial phase of the design process, I conducted in-depth research to understand user needs and preferences while also analyzing the competitive landscape. This dual approach helped me gather valuable insights from multiple sources, which guided the design decisions for the Gourmety app.

User Research Insights

To better understand the needs and preferences of potential users, I conducted user research using a combination of self-led interviews and empathy practices. Through these interviews, I explored questions such as cooking frequency, challenges faced in meal preparation, and desired features in a cooking app. Key insights from the research include:

  • Time Constraints: "I often feel pressed for time when it comes to cooking, especially during busy weekdays."
  • Limited Access to Ingredients and Equipment: "Getting fresh ingredients and having access to kitchen equipment can be tough, especially in shared living spaces."
  • Preference for Simplified Recipes: "I like recipes that are easy, require minimal ingredients, and can be adapted to my dietary preferences."

Competitive Analysis

In parallel with user research, I conducted a competitive analysis to evaluate existing cooking apps and identify opportunities for differentiation. Through this analysis, I assessed competitors' strengths, weaknesses, gaps, and opportunities. Key findings from the competitive analysis include:

  • Competitors' Strengths: Existing cooking apps offer a wide range of recipes, user-friendly interfaces, and social features like recipe sharing and meal planning. Notably, apps such as Cookpad and Tasty have successfully integrated features that allow users to share food photos, fostering a sense of community and inspiration.
  • Competitors' Weaknesses: Many competitors, however, lack robust filtering options for specific constraints such as cookware, cooking time, and dietary preferences. This gap presents an opportunity to offer a more personalized experience for users with unique needs.
  • Gaps and Opportunities: There is also a noticeable gap in the market for a cooking app tailored to teenagers, addressing their unique challenges and preferences. By focusing on this demographic and integrating features like simplified recipes and kitchen equipment filtering, Gourmety can effectively differentiate itself in the market.

Define

In the Define phase, I created personas and user journey maps to better understand the target audience's needs, behaviors, and preferences, drawing on insights gathered during user interviews.

Persona

User Journey Map

Marcus' Journey

  1. Marcus starts his culinary journey by searching for recipes using the Gourmety app.
  2. He navigates through the search results, using filters to find recipes based on available cooking tools and ingredients.
  3. Marcus selects a recipe that suits his preferences and begins cooking in his school dormitory kitchen.
  4. He follows the recipe instructions step-by-step, making the most of the limited equipment and ingredients he has.
  5. After preparing the meal, Marcus shares his cooking success on the Gourmety app and receives encouraging feedback from the community.

Queena's Journey

  1. Queena opens the Gourmety app during a break from her busy schedule.
  2. She explores the latest articles and cooking tips on the app's homepage, looking for inspiration for her next meal.
  3. Queena uses the search function to find quick, nutritious recipes that match her dietary preferences and time constraints.
  4. She saves her favorite recipes to her collection for future reference and adds the necessary ingredients to her shopping list.
  5. Queena prepares a healthy meal following the recipe instructions and enjoys a satisfying, nutritious dining experience.

Persona Insights

  • Through the development of personas, common themes such as the importance of convenience, nutrition, and simplicity in meal preparation came to light.
  • Interestingly, both Marcus and Queena expressed a strong desire for community engagement and the opportunity to share their cooking experiences with others on the Gourmety app.

User Needs and Goals

  • The primary needs of Marcus and Queena focus on accessing convenient, nutritious, and easy-to-follow recipes that cater to their specific constraints and preferences.
  • Both personas are looking for a user-friendly platform that offers personalized recommendations and fosters a sense of community through engagement and interaction.

Impact on Design Decisions

  • The personas and user journey maps played a key role in shaping the design decisions for the Gourmety app, ensuring its features and functionalities align with the needs and goals of the target users.
  • Insights derived from personas influenced the development of tailored features such as the "What’s in my kitchen" filter and the community sharing functionality, enhancing the overall user experience.

This section highlights a variety of ideation activities aimed at sparking innovation and uncovering unique solutions to enhance the user experience of our recipe-search platform.

From user flows and site maps to rapid sketching and ideation sessions, each activity contributes significantly to shaping the design direction and refining our concepts. Come along as we explore different creative approaches to design an engaging and intuitive cooking experience for our users.

Ideation Activities

Crazy Eight

The Crazy Eight activity is a rapid ideation technique where participants sketch eight different design concepts within a short timeframe. This exercise sparks creativity and encourages the exploration of diverse ideas, helping to generate a wide range of potential solutions.

  1. Smart Recipe Search Engine: Find recipes based on available ingredients.
  2. Cookware Selection Cards: Quickly choose the right cookware for your recipes with intuitive selection cards.
  3. Filter by Ingredient, Cookware, and Time: Easily refine recipe options to match your needs.
  4. Virtual Assistant in Full-Screen Recipe: Navigate recipes seamlessly with a virtual assistant. Control the flow by moving to the previous step, advancing to the next, or marking the recipe as done.
  5. Enhanced Recipe Cards: Preview key details like ingredients, required cookware, and cooking time at a glance.
  6. "Kitchen" Immersive Experience: Step into a virtual kitchen where you can explore recipes, ingredients, and cookware options. Dive deeper into culinary ideas with articles on cookware and ingredient uses.
  7. Organized Collections: Manage your recipes with sections like "Up Next" and "Done" for better organization.

How Might We...?

The HMW questions are used to reframe challenges as opportunities for creative solutions. They serve as a guide for the ideation process, posing provocative questions that inspire innovative thinking and problem-solving.

  • How might we turn the limited-tooling situation into a fun experience?
  • Introduce interactive cooking tutorials within the platform to guide users through cooking techniques using basic utensils and ingredients in an engaging way. These tutorials can feature dynamic, step-by-step instructions, practical tips, and interactive elements, transforming the cooking process into an enjoyable and accessible activity. Adding gamification elements, such as challenges, rewards, and achievements, can further enhance the fun and immersive experience for users with limited tools.
  • How might we provide cooking options for users with limited kitchen tools?
  • Develop a search engine feature that suggests recipes based on available kitchen utensils, ensuring users can easily find recipes that match their tool inventory. This feature would analyze user input on available tools and recommend recipes requiring minimal or alternative equipment, making cooking accessible and inclusive.
  • How might a virtual assistant help with cooking?
  • Implement a virtual assistant feature to guide users through recipes step-by-step, offering voice-controlled navigation for hands-free convenience. This assistant could provide helpful cooking tips, suggest ingredient substitutions, and answer culinary questions in real time, boosting user confidence and efficiency in the kitchen.

Design

In the design phase of the Gourmety app development process, I focused on transforming user insights and requirements into practical design solutions. This phase involved crafting wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes to bring the app's user interface to life and refine its functionality.

Wireframes

I began the design process by creating wireframes, which served as a blueprint for the app's layout and interaction flow. These wireframes outlined the key screens and interactions, including the search screen, search results, and recipe pages. Through iterative sketching and refinement, I ensured that the wireframes accurately captured the core features and user flow of the app.

Search Screen Sketch

One of the primary screens I designed was the search screen, which serves as the central hub for recipe discovery. Here, users can input their search criteria and explore a wide range of recipes tailored to their preferences. To streamline the user experience, I enhanced the "What’s in my kitchen" feature as a filter function, simplifying the search process while introducing an immersive background design to maintain visual engagement.

Search Result Sketch

The search result screen displays a curated list of recipes based on the user's search criteria. I ensured design coherence by incorporating a consistent curved design motif throughout the interface, reinforcing brand identity and visual harmony. Each search result preview includes essential information such as cooking time, difficulty level, and user ratings, allowing users to make informed decisions about which recipe to explore further.

Recipe Page Sketch

The recipe page is where users can view detailed instructions and ingredients for a selected recipe. I introduced interactive elements such as checkbox options for ingredient selection and a "check all" button for added convenience. Additionally, I refined the instructional section by adding a "full-screen" button and designated areas for instructional photos, enhancing the page’s usability and user engagement.

Other Screens and Low-Fidelity Prototype

In addition to the search and recipe pages, I sketched wireframes for other essential screens, including Today, Collection, Kitchen, Shopping List, and Account screens. These wireframes offered a comprehensive overview of the app's functionality and user flow. I then developed these wireframes into low-fidelity prototypes, enabling stakeholders to interact with the app’s basic functionality and flow.

Test

Now, let's see how we evaluated whether our designs truly worked. We conducted usability tests to assess how easy the app is to use and identified opportunities for improvement.

Methodology

For the usability testing phase of the Gourmety app, I employed a combination of self-testing and a single-participant test to evaluate the prototype's usability and uncover areas for enhancement.

Self-Test

As a preliminary step, I conducted a self-test to navigate through the prototype and identify any initial usability issues or inconsistencies. This process provided firsthand experience with the app’s functionality and interface, allowing me to make initial observations before involving external participants.

Single-Participant Testing

Following the self-test, I conducted a usability test with a single participant to gather additional feedback and insights. The participant, a representative of our target demographic—young people aged 12–25 with cooking experience—was tasked with completing actions such as recipe discovery and navigation within the app. The test session lasted approximately 10–15 minutes and was conducted remotely.

Insights and Recommendations

Key findings from both the self-test and participant testing highlighted areas for improvement, including navigation clarity, information coherence, and icon clarity. These insights provided actionable recommendations to refine and enhance the prototype, ensuring a more seamless user experience.

Iteration and UI Design

After reviewing the usability test, I identified three critical areas for improvement: navigation clarity, information coherence, and icon clarity. These insights directly influenced the adjustments made during the UI design phase, ensuring a more seamless and user-friendly experience.

UI Design Sticker Sheet

Navigation Clarity

Based on insights from the usability test, I noticed that the interaction could be confusing for many users. To address this, I introduced a 'Recipe' button, which provides clearer guidance and enhances users' understanding of the app's functionality.

Information Coherence

The usability test highlighted the importance of maintaining consistency across different app interfaces. As a result, I added a cookware label and a 'like' button to the recipe page, in addition to the search result page. This ensures a more cohesive user experience and improves navigation and interaction.

Icon Clarity

During the UI design stage, I made several adjustments to enhance icon clarity and improve the overall user experience. This included revising the navigation tab icons and introducing a search icon to the search bar. I replaced the day-check symbol with a newspaper icon and renamed the 'Today' page to the 'News' page for better icon representation. Additionally, I added titles beneath the icons to further improve user understanding and navigation within the app.

Takeaway

  1. Prioritize User Experience: By conducting thorough user research and usability testing, we were able to identify areas for improvement and make informed design decisions to enhance the overall user experience.
  2. Iterative Design Process: Our iterative design process allowed us to continually refine and optimize the Gourmety app prototype based on user feedback and insights gathered at each stage of development.
  3. Focus on Clarity and Consistency: Enhancements focused on improving navigation clarity, information consistency, and icon clarity, ensuring that users can easily navigate the app and consistently understand its functionality.
  4. User-Centric Design: The personas and user journey maps created during the design process helped us empathize with our target users, tailoring the app's features to meet their specific needs and preferences.
  5. Continuous Improvement: Usability testing served as a valuable tool for validating design decisions and identifying areas for further refinement, emphasizing the importance of ongoing iteration and improvement throughout the design process.
  6. Empowering User Engagement: Features such as the "Recipe" button, cookware labels, and like buttons are designed to empower users to engage more effectively with the app, fostering a sense of ownership and satisfaction in their culinary exploration journey.
